Visual Hierarchy Hick's Law PARC: Contrast

Old Navy

Visual Hierarchy is the idea that a webpage should be structured in a way that presents content in the order of importance. The first content should be eye catching and should draw the viewer in. In this example, Old Navy does a great job at presenting deals and limited time offers that they hope the shopper utilize. Moheim

Hick's Law is a function that measures the reaction time that we have to stimuli. In web design this is important because if there is too much stimuli, the reaction time is greater. Hick's law is the idea that less is more. You don't want to overwhelm the viewer and so you should give them less options and make the website simple. Moheim is a japanese retailer whose website is minimalistic - much like their products. It is not only fitting not only for their services, but also for the message they are giving their customers. The Empathy Experiment

Contrast is one of the PARC principles. Constrast helps bring the user's attention to certain details and brings not only beauty, but utility to the website as well. The Empathy Experiment utilizes this principle flawlessly and has vibrant colors that help to enrich the user experience and bring awareness to key points.
